Grammer: Pronouns

Learning Goal:

By the end of the lesson, you will be able to identify and use pronouns correctly

There Are Many Types of Pronouns

There are many types of pronouns, they replace nouns and other pronouns. We use them in our speech and writing in order to avoid repetition and make the sentences less bulky. Consider the following sentence:

Jerry couldn't decide which way he wanted to face his chair; but if he faced it toward the window, he would get too much sun.

Now consider it as it would read without pronouns:

Jerry couldn't decide which way Jerry wanted to face Jerry's chair; but if Jerry faced the chair toward the window, Jerry would get too much sun.

The rules

Pronouns must agree in number and person.

  • Example of agreement in number:
    • If the teachers wanted the students to complete the project, they should have given them more time

Teachers is a plural noun, so the pronoun to replace teachers must be plural as well. The word teachers is replaced by the pronoun "they".

  • Example of agreement in person:
    • Mark loves to swim, so he built a pool in his backyard.

The noun Mark is replaced by the pronoun that represents a singular male, he.

Remember, the pronouns need to refer to a specific noun. Make sure you aren't vague, or the reader won't know what you are referring to in your sentence.

  • Example:
    • The car hit the telephone pole. It wasn't damaged though.

It is referring to what, the car or the telephone pole?

Why Do I Need To Know

The biggest problem with pronouns is getting them to agree with the nouns they replace. Here are some of the biggest problems that you hear frequently:

1. If a parent is worried about school lunches being nutritious, they can call the school for the menu.

We often get so concerned about using the pronoun he to refer to all nouns with unknown gender, it is common to replace it with the non-gender pronoun ''they''. The problem with this is that a parent is clearly singular, and they is plural. You could change the noun to plural and that would correct it easily:

Correct: If parents are worried about school lunches being nutritious, they can call the school for the menu.

2. Every item on the list had their own description.

Some collective pronouns may appear to be plural, but they are really singular.

The following pronouns are singular and take a singular pronoun:

Every, everyone, either, neither, everybody, somebody, nobody, anybody, anyone.

Correct: Every item on the list had its own description.

Pronouns as subjects

He, she, it, we, you, they and I are pronouns that may be used as the subject of a sentence. Sometimes a pronoun is one of two subjects.

EXAMPLE:

- We sent to the school early.
- John and he played ball.
- He and I played ball.
